Tracking renewables tripling and fossil fuels phaseout

In 2023, at COP28, governments agreed to triple global renewable capacity by 2030.

Tripling renewables capacity to at least 11,000 GW by 2030 keeps a pathway for limiting global warming to 1.5C within reach, according to independent analyses from the International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA) and the International Energy Agency (IEA).

Tripling renewables is possibly the most powerful action the world can take within the next decade to transition away from fossil fuels, accounting for up to two-thirds of emissions reductions by 2050.

Global Energy Monitor’s tracking of energy facilities across the sector provides a detailed view of progress toward this critical goal.
